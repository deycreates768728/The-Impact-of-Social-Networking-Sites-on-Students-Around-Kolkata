The Impact of Social Networking Sites on Students Around Kolkata
A statistics survey project examining how social media usage affects academic performance, screen time, distraction, and behavior among students in and around Kolkata, based on a 140-respondent questionnaire and analyzed in R.
Authors: Manish Chandra Roy (22214120027), Sourav Dey (22214110028), Swarnendu Dey (22214110024), Roshni Majhi (22214220029)
Program: Department of Statistics, Presidency University, Kolkata, West Bengal, India
Overview
The rise of social networking sites (SNS) has reshaped how students interact, learn, and manage their time — a trend accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic. This project investigates that impact through an original survey and statistical analysis.
Research questions covered:
General demographics of respondents
Effects of frequent social media use on students
Relationship between platform choice and daily screen time
Entertainment vs. educational use of social media
Impact of social media "addiction" on academic performance
Distraction from social media and coping strategies
Noticeable behavioral changes from heavy use
Methodology: Online questionnaire survey (Google Forms), simple random sampling, descriptive statistics, and inferential tests (chi-square tests of independence) on 140 cleaned responses covering demographics, usage habits, reading behavior, satisfaction ratings, and sleep patterns.
Key findings:
Instagram, WhatsApp, and YouTube are the most-used platforms
A significant relationship was found between daily internet usage hours and academic satisfaction level (chi-square test, p = 0.024)
No significant relationship was found between stream of study and time spent on social networking sites, locality and internet usage hours, or purpose of use and stream of study
Most respondents sleep 6–8 hours per day; higher-income respondents tend to spend more time online
